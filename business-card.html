Why Use a QR Code for Business Card?

Traditional business cards often end up in drawers or trash cans. A QR code business card ensures your contact information is saved directly to the recipient's phone. When scanned, the QR code can open a digital vCard, your LinkedIn profile, or a landing page with all your links.

QR codes on business cards are especially valuable at networking events where you meet dozens of people. Instead of manually typing contact details, new connections simply scan your card and save everything instantly. This increases follow-up rates significantly.

How It Works

Create a simple webpage with your contact details, or use a vCard generator to create a .vcf file URL. Paste this URL into the generator above, click the button, and download your QR code. Add it to your printed business cards, email signature, or LinkedIn banner.

Best Practices

  • Keep the QR code at least 2x2 cm for reliable scanning
  • Place it on the back of your business card to not interfere with design
  • Include a short call-to-action like 'Scan to save contact'
  • Test the QR code on both light and dark backgrounds before printing

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QR code business card?
A QR code business card is a traditional printed card with a scannable QR code that, when scanned, saves your contact details to the recipient's phone or opens your digital profile. It bridges the gap between physical networking and digital connections.
What information should my QR code business card contain?
The QR code can link to a vCard (.vcf file) that includes your name, phone, email, company, title, website, and social media links. Alternatively, link to a personal landing page with all your contact options.
How do I add a QR code to my printed business cards?
Generate your QR code on PlainQR, download the PNG image, and provide it to your business card designer or print shop. Most designers can easily integrate a QR code into any card layout.
Can people save my contact details automatically?
Yes, if you link to a vCard (.vcf) file, most phones will offer to save the contact directly. If you link to a webpage, include a 'Add to Contacts' button that downloads the vCard.
